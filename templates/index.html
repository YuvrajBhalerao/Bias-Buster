The Hidden Risks in Your Data

Even with the best intentions, bias can silently creep into datasets, leading to models that are unfair, unreliable, and untrustworthy. We help you find the three most common culprits.

Class Imbalance

When models see one group far more often than another, they become biased towards the majority. This leads to poor performance and unfair outcomes for underrepresented groups.

Feature Skewness

Skewed data can cause a model to over-value certain ranges of a feature, creating a system that is unfairly optimized for a specific subset of your data.

Missing Data

If data isn't missing at random, it creates blind spots. Your model may learn from an incomplete picture, leading to biased and unreliable conclusions.
